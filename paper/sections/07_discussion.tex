\section{Discussion and conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Scope and limitations.}
\GeoGenBench measures whether a model can translate a textual specification of a 2D plane-geometry construction into a globally-consistent metric realisation; it does not test proof construction, 3D solids, coordinate-plane algebra, or \emph{teaching utility}.
$35\%$ of templated scenarios are loose: a passing verdict means ``produced \emph{a} diagram consistent with the prompt's stated constraints,'' not \emph{the} canonical diagram.
Two \structured\ cells are marginally partial (\opus\ $574/600$, \gpt\ $545/600$) due to LLM-IR circular-dependency cycles --- conservative against those pathologies.
On the three cells run on curriculum at headline N, \haiku\ + \rawcode\ drops $82.1\% \to 36.0\%$, \haiku\ + \structured\ $84.5\% \to 36.8\%$, \geminiflash\ + \rawcode\ $39.4\% \to 25.4\%$: the curriculum split is materially harder for every cell measured (topical breadth and LLM-authored predicate lists both contribute).
Headline-run findings are templated-split findings.

\paragraph{Threats to validity.}
The procedural template engine ships with the benchmark, allowing reviewers to roll fresh contamination-free splits.
The adversarial probe found 5 predicate soft spots all addressable by hardening or template-side mitigation, yielding $<5\%$ vulnerability to constructed adversarial examples; the human-correlation study (\cref{ssec:human}) found a larger $\sim 18\%$ rate of \emph{naturally-occurring} model outputs the verifier accepts but expert raters reject, concentrated on auxiliary-construction templates whose property lists do not enforce prompt-specified numerics.

\paragraph{Deployment and conclusion.}
For raw TikZ, \gpt + \rawcode\ dominates (beats \opus\ + \rawcode\ by $20$\pp at $4.5\times$ lower cost; \haiku\ + \rawcode\ tied with \opus\ + \rawcode\ at $14\times$ lower cost).
At the high-quality end, the three frontier+\structured\ cells converge within $1$\pp at $7\times$ cost spread; \gpt + \structured\ is the unique Pareto corner.
\GeoGen's two pillars (\cref{sec:design}) extend beyond plane geometry: procedural co-generation reuses wherever a domain admits a Python-expressible construction, and render-then-symbolic-verify extends along the predicate-language axis (versioned, additive).
Two extensions are in flight: a GenExam-Math integration \citep{wang2025genexam} re-encoding a 91-prompt geometry subset for empirical comparison between automatic verification and vision-LM judges, and a coordinate-plane / 3D-solid extension.
We release the dataset, template engine, verification toolkit, and Docker renderer under permissive licenses.
